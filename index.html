Architecture
How it actually works.
No hand-waving. Here's the technical architecture.
L1

Settlement Layer

ERC-20 on Ethereum mainnet. Final settlement of all transactions. Hourly batch settlement from payment channels. Multi-sig treasury. Governance contract for proposals and voting.

L2

Payment Channels

Off-chain payment channels for high-frequency agent transactions. Millisecond settlement. Sub-cent fees. State channels commit to L1 hourly. Dispute resolution built in.

$$

Stripe Bridge

Fiat on/off ramp via Stripe. Buy ROAD with credit card. Sell ROAD to bank account. Real-time rate oracle. KYC handled by Stripe. No exchange account needed.

// Architecture flow
//
// Agent A needs compute from Agent B:
// 1. Agent A opens payment channel with Agent B (one-time, L1)
// 2. Agent A sends 0.004 ROAD per inference request (off-chain, instant)
// 3. After 1000 requests: 4 ROAD accumulated in channel
// 4. Hourly settlement: channel state committed to L1
// 5. Agent B cashes out: 4 ROAD -> bridge.sell() -> $4.00 to bank
//
// Total on-chain transactions: 2 (open channel + settle)
// Total off-chain transactions: 1000
// Gas cost: ~$0.50 total (not per transaction)
Governance
Your tokens, your vote.
ROAD holders govern the treasury and protocol upgrades. One token, one vote. Delegation supported. BlackRoad OS Inc. has no veto power.

Proposal Process

Any holder with 100,000+ ROAD (or delegated votes) can submit a proposal. 7-day voting period. 10% quorum required. Simple majority passes. Timelock delay before execution for security review.

What Can Be Governed

Treasury spending (grants, bounties, partnerships). Protocol fee adjustments. Bridge parameters. Ecosystem fund distribution. The governance contract itself (upgradeable via vote).

Delegation

Don't want to vote on every proposal? Delegate your voting power to someone you trust. Revoke anytime. Delegation is free (off-chain signature). Your tokens stay in your wallet.

Transparency

Every proposal, vote, and treasury transaction is on-chain and publicly auditable. No back-room deals. No secret allocations. The block explorer shows everything.

FAQ
Straight answers.

Why not just use ETH or USDC for agent payments?

Gas costs. Every ETH/USDC transfer costs gas. When agents transact thousands of times per second at sub-cent amounts, gas fees would exceed the payment value. ROAD uses off-chain payment channels with periodic on-chain settlement, reducing gas costs by 99%+.

Is ROAD required to use BlackRoad OS?

No. You can use BlackRoad OS entirely with fiat payments via RoadPay. ROAD is optional and exists specifically for agent micropayments and creator payouts where fiat rails are too slow or expensive. If Stripe works for your use case, use Stripe.

Can I cash out ROAD to real dollars?

Yes. The Stripe bridge converts ROAD to USD and deposits to your bank account. No exchange account needed. No withdrawal delays. Standard Stripe Connect payout timing (1-2 business days).

Will ROAD be listed on exchanges?

Not until legal review is complete and we're confident in compliance. Our priority is utility (agent payments, creator payouts), not trading. The Stripe bridge provides liquidity without exchange dependency.

What if the SEC classifies ROAD as a security?

We've designed ROAD to meet utility token criteria (usable from day one, no promise of profit, decentralized governance). If regulation changes, we'll adapt. The Stripe bridge ensures ROAD holders can always convert to USD. We're building legally, with counsel.

Why should I trust you when 80% of crypto projects failed?

Because we're not a crypto project that happens to have software. We're a software company (BlackRoad OS) that needs a programmable payment layer for AI agents. The token serves the software, not the other way around. If ROAD disappeared tomorrow, BlackRoad OS would still work -- just with Stripe only. The token makes agent micropayments possible, not the company viable.

How do agent micropayments actually work?

Two agents open a payment channel (one on-chain transaction). Then they transact off-chain at any speed and any amount. Periodically (hourly by default), the channel state is committed on-chain. This means 1000 micropayments cost the same gas as 1 transaction. The math works.

What's stopping the team from dumping their tokens?

1-year cliff and 4-year vesting, enforced by smart contract (not a promise). Team tokens are locked on-chain. After the cliff, tokens vest linearly. You can verify this on Etherscan. Also: 15% team allocation is standard (below industry average of 20-25%).
